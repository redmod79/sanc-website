**Key Findings from Related Studies:**

**From day-of-atonement-chiastic-structure/CONCLUSION.md:**
- Lev 16 exhibits a clear chiastic (mirror) structure: A(wash/garments v.4) - B(kill bullock v.6,11) - C(enter MHP v.12-14) - D(LORD's goat v.15) - E(atone for holy place v.16) - X(NO MAN v.17) - E'(atone for altar v.18-19) - D'("made an end" v.20a) - C'(return v.23) - B'(burnt offerings v.24-25) - A'(wash/garments v.24)
- The scapegoat comes AFTER atonement is complete (v.20b-22) — it is REMOVAL, not atonement
- "No man in the tabernacle" (v.17) is the CENTER/PIVOT of the entire ritual
- Sevenfold sprinkling on mercy seat (v.14) and altar (v.19) indicates completeness
- Aftermath: scapegoat to wilderness (v.20b-22) + sin offerings burned outside camp (v.27-28)
- The DOA maps to Revelation: Trumpets = warnings; Bowls = judgment; "No man" (Lev 16:17) = Rev 15:8; "Made an end" = "It is done" (Rev 16:17); Scapegoat = Satan bound (Rev 20:1-3)
- Three levels of chiasm identified: the DOA service itself, the Seventh Month festivals, and Revelation's structure

**From atonement-meaning/CONCLUSION.md:**
- KPR root family: kaphar (H3722, verb, "to cover") → kippur (H3725, institution) → kapporeth (H3727, place) → kopher (H3724, cost/ransom)
- LEV 17:11 is the foundational atonement text: blood = life; God gave it for atonement; blood is the designated agent
- Atonement is substitutionary: hand-laying transfers guilt (LEV 1:4), scapegoat confession (LEV 16:21)
- Both propitiation (God-ward, satisfying justice: ROM 3:25-26) and reconciliation (man-ward: ROM 5:10)
- LXX bridge: H3727 kapporeth → G2435 hilasterion (mercy seat → propitiation in ROM 3:25)
- Christ IS the mercy seat (ROM 3:25, hilasterion) — the convergence of God's presence, atoning blood, and the law
- Old system was typological: "shadow of good things to come" (HEB 10:1); annual repetition proved insufficiency

**From rev-15-8-smoke-inauguration-vs-judgment/CONCLUSION.md:**
- Rev 15:8 is a composite allusion fusing inauguration glory (Exo 40:34-35) with DOA exclusion (Lev 16:17) and Isaianic theophany (Isa 6:4)
- Key parallel: Lev 16:17 "no man (kol adam) shall be in the tabernacle...until he come out" → Rev 15:8 "no man was able to enter...till the seven plagues were fulfilled"
- The DOA exclusion was a PROHIBITION; Rev 15:8 is an INABILITY — the heavenly antitype intensifies the type
- The vessel transformation arc: golden bowls of prayer (Rev 5:8) become bowls of wrath (Rev 15:7) — same vessels, different contents
- "Temple of the tabernacle of the testimony" (Rev 15:5) identifies judgment as proceeding from the law in the ark
- The three phases of sanctuary access: (1) inauguration, (2) daily ministry/intercession, (3) DOA/judgment — Rev 15:8 = phase 3

**From sanc-08-veil-access/CONCLUSION.md:**
- Paroketh (H6532) = "separatrix" — active participle meaning "that which separates"
- Veil with cherubim connects to Gen 3:24 — sanctuary version of the Edenic barrier
- LEV 16:2 uses jussive negation (ve'al yavo) — direct prohibition; "that he die not"; context = death of Nadab and Abihu (LEV 16:1)
- DOA entry required: white linen garments (v.4), incense cloud for protection (v.12-13, "that he die not"), blood sprinkled on mercy seat (v.14-15)
- Exclusion formula (Lev 16:17): "no man in the tabernacle" — solitary, temporary, must be repeated
- Christ's entry is INAUGURATION (enkainizo, HEB 10:20), not DOA repetition: permanent, representative (prodromos/forerunner, HEB 6:20), inaugurating
