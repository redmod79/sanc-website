## kaphar (כָּפַר) — H3722
**Original:** כָּפַר
**Transliteration:** kâphar (kaw-far')

## kapporeth (כַּפֹּרֶת) — H3727
**Original:** כַּפֹּרֶת
**Transliteration:** kappôreth (kap-po'-reth)
**Part of Speech:** Feminine noun
**BLB Count:** 27

### Definition
From H3722; a lid (used only of the cover of the sacred Ark): mercy seat. Derived from kaphar — literally "the covering" or "the place of covering/atonement."

### Translations
- "the mercy seat" — 22x (81.5%)
- "of the mercy seat" — 3x (11.1%)
- "a mercy seat" — 1x
- "the mercy seatward" — 1x

### Leviticus 16 Occurrences
- 16:2 — "before the mercy seat" / "upon the mercy seat" (2x)
- 16:13 — "cover the mercy seat"
- 16:14 — "upon the mercy seat eastward" / "before the mercy seat" (2x)
- 16:15 — "upon the mercy seat, and before the mercy seat" (2x)

### LXX Connection
H3727 kapporeth -> G2435 ἱλαστήριον (hilasterion) — "an expiatory (place/thing)" (PMI: 9.75, highest relevance)
- This is the same word used in ROM 3:25 where Christ is called "hilasterion" — propitiation/mercy seat
- The LXX bridge: kapporeth (OT mercy seat) = hilasterion (NT propitiation) = Christ (ROM 3:25)
- Also: G2787 κιβωτός (kibotos) "ark" (PMI: 5.85), G2665 καταπέτασμα (katapetasma) "veil" (PMI: 7.44)

## nazah (נָזָה) — H5137
**Original:** נָזָה
**Transliteration:** nâzâh (naw-zaw')
**Part of Speech:** Verb
**BLB Count:** 24

### Definition
A primitive root; to spirt, i.e. besprinkle (especially in expiation): sprinkle.

### Translations
- "and sprinkle" — 8x (28.6%)
- "And he shall sprinkle" — 3x
- "shall sprinkle" / "shall he sprinkle" — 2x each
- Various sprinkle forms — remainder

### Leviticus 16 Occurrences
- 16:14 — הִזָּה (hizzah, Hiphil perf) — "and sprinkle" (on mercy seat eastward)
- 16:14 — יַזֶּה (yazzeh, Hiphil impf) — "shall he sprinkle" (seven times before mercy seat)
- 16:15 — הִזָּה (hizzah, Hiphil perf) — "and sprinkle" (goat blood on/before mercy seat)
- 16:19 — הִזָּה (implied) — "shall sprinkle" (seven times on altar)

### Key Pattern
The verb is always in the Hiphil (causative) stem in Lev 16, meaning "to cause to spatter/sprinkle." The sevenfold sprinkling (sheba' pe'amim) appears in v.14 and v.19, indicating completeness. This same verb appears in ISA 52:15 — "so shall he sprinkle many nations" — connecting the DOA sprinkling to the Suffering Servant.

## taher (טָהֵר) — H2891
**Original:** טָהֵר
**Transliteration:** ṭâhêr (taw-hare')
**Part of Speech:** Verb
**BLB Count:** 94

### Definition
A primitive root; properly, to be bright; i.e. (by implication) to be pure (physically sound, clear, unadulterated; Levitically, uncontaminated; morally, innocent or holy): be (make, make self, pronounce) clean, cleanse (self), purge, purify(-ier, self).

### Leviticus 16 Occurrences
- 16:19 — "and cleanse it" — the altar cleansed from uncleanness
- 16:30 — "to cleanse you, [that] ye may be clean" (2x) — Piel infinitive construct + Qal imperfect — the PURPOSE of atonement: moral/ceremonial purification

### Key Verse: Lev 16:30
Hebrew: לְטַהֵר אֶתְכֶם מִכֹּל חַטֹּאתֵיכֶם לִפְנֵי יְהוָה תִּטְהָרוּ
"to cleanse (Piel inf. con.) you from all your sins; before YHWH you shall be clean (Qal impf.)"
- The Piel (intensive) form shows God actively purifying
- The Qal (simple) shows the resulting state: they SHALL BE clean

## anah (עָנָה) — H6031
**Original:** עָנָה
**Transliteration:** ʻânâh (aw-naw')
**Part of Speech:** Verb
**BLB Count:** 84

### Definition
A primitive root; to depress literally or figuratively, transitive or intransitive: abase self, afflict(-ion, self), answer (by mistake), chasten self, deal hardly with, defile, exercise, force, gentleness, humble (self), hurt, ravish, sing (by mistake), submit self, weaken.

### Translations
- "afflict" — 5x
- "was afflicted" — 3x
- "to afflict" — 2x
- "to humble" — 1x
- Also: force, humble, submit, chasten, weaken

### Day of Atonement Occurrences
- LEV 16:29 — תְּעַנּוּ (Piel impf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"
- LEV 16:31 — וְעִנִּיתֶם (Piel perf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"
- LEV 23:27 — וְעִנִּיתֶם (Piel perf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"
- LEV 23:29 — תְעֻנֶּה (Pual impf. 3fs) "shall not be afflicted" — PASSIVE: the soul that is not afflicted
- LEV 23:32 — וְעִנִּיתֶם (Piel perf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"
- NUM 29:7 — תְּעַנּוּ (Piel impf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"

### Key Notes
The Piel stem (intensive active) is used for the command: actively, intentionally afflict. The Pual (intensive passive) in LEV 23:29 describes the person who fails to BE afflicted. The expression "afflict your souls" (te'annu et-nafshotekem) is unique to the Day of Atonement among all feast days. The word combines physical affliction (fasting) with spiritual humbling.

## karath (כָּרַת) — H3772
**Original:** כָּרַת
**Transliteration:** kârath (kaw-rath')
**Part of Speech:** Verb
**BLB Count:** 288

### Definition
A primitive root; to cut (off, down or asunder); by implication, to destroy or consume; specifically, to covenant (i.e. make an alliance or bargain, originally by cutting flesh and passing between the pieces): be chewed, be con-(feder-)ate, covenant, cut (down, off), destroy, fail, feller, be freed, hew (down), make a league (covenant), lose, perish, utterly, want.

### Translations
- "shall be cut off" — 30x (8.4%)

- "fail" — 6x

### Day of Atonement Usage
- LEV 23:29 — וְנִכְרְתָה (Niphal perf. 3fs) "shall be cut off from among his people"
- This is the karat penalty for failure to afflict one's soul on the Day of Atonement

### Leviticus "Cut Off" Penalties (broader context)
LEV 7:20,21,25,27; 17:4,9,10,14; 18:29; 19:8; 20:3,5,6,17,18; 22:3; 23:29 — The "cut off" penalty appears throughout Leviticus for various violations, but its appearance in LEV 23:29 makes the DOA uniquely severe among annual festivals.

## azazel (עֲזָאזֵל) — H5799
**Original:** עֲזָאזֵל
**Transliteration:** ʻăzâʼzêl (az-aw-zale')
**Part of Speech:** Masculine noun (treated as proper noun in Hebrew parser)
**BLB Count:** 4

### Definition
From H5795 (ez, goat) and H235 (azal, to go away); goat of departure; the scapegoat: scapegoat.

### All Occurrences (4 total, all in Leviticus 16)
- LEV 16:8 — "the other lot for the scapegoat" (la-azazel)
- LEV 16:10 — "to be the scapegoat...for a scapegoat" (la-azazel, 2x)
- LEV 16:26 — "the goat for the scapegoat" (la-azazel)

### Hebrew Parser Notes
The Hebrew parser treats עֲזָאזֵל as a proper noun (PropN.ms.Abs), suggesting it may be a name rather than a description. Two main interpretations:
1. "Goat of departure" (ez + azal) — describing the goat's function
2. A proper name for a demonic entity sent to the wilderness — the goat is sent "to Azazel"

In Lev 16:8, the parallel structure is significant: "one lot for YHWH (la-YHWH) and one lot for Azazel (la-azazel)" — the preposition la- ("to/for") is identical for both, suggesting Azazel is a personal entity (parallel to YHWH as a proper name), not merely a description of the goat's function.

## goral (גּוֹרָל) — H1486
**Original:** גּוֹרָל
**Transliteration:** gôwrâl (go-rawl')
**Part of Speech:** Masculine noun
**BLB Count:** 77

### Definition
From an unused root meaning to be rough (as stone); properly, a pebble, i.e. a lot (small stones being used for that purpose); figuratively, a portion or destiny (as if determined by lot): lot.

### Translations
- "lot" — 16x (17.8%)
- "lots" — 15x (16.7%)
- "by lot" — 15x (16.7%)
- "the lot" — 13x (14.4%)

### Leviticus 16 Occurrences
- 16:8 — goralot (lots, mp) — cast upon two goats
- 16:8 — goral echad (one lot) for YHWH, goral echad (one lot) for Azazel
- 16:9 — ha-goral (the lot) — upon which YHWH's lot fell
- 16:10 — ha-goral (the lot) — which fell for Azazel

### Key Note
The lot-casting removes human choice from the designation. It is God who determines which goat is for YHWH and which for Azazel (cf. PRO 16:33 — "The lot is cast into the lap; but the whole disposing thereof is of the LORD").

## bad (בַּד) — H906
**Original:** בַּד
**Transliteration:** bad (bad)
**Part of Speech:** Masculine noun
**BLB Count:** 23

### Definition
Perhaps from H909 (in the sense of divided fibres); flaxen thread or yarn; hence, a linen garment: linen.

### Translations
- "linen" — 14x (60.9%)
- "in linen" — 3x
- "the linen" — 2x
- "a linen" — 2x

### Leviticus 16 Occurrences
- 16:4 — "linen coat" (kethoneth-bad), "linen breeches" (miknese-bad), "linen girdle" (abnet bad), "linen mitre" (mitsnefeth bad) — 4 occurrences, one for each garment
- 16:23 — "the linen garments" (bigdei ha-bad) — put off after entering Most Holy Place
- 16:32 — "the linen clothes" (bigdei ha-bad) — worn for future atonement

### Key Note
Bad (linen) garments are worn specifically for the Day of Atonement, REPLACING the normal golden garments of the high priest (EXO 28:2-43). White linen signifies purity and humility. The same word describes the garments of the man with the writer's inkhorn in EZK 9:2,3,11; 10:2,6,7 and the man in Daniel's vision (DAN 10:5; 12:6,7). REV 15:6 describes angels in "pure and white linen" — connecting to DOA white linen garments.

## qetoreth (קְטֹרֶת) — H7004
**Original:** קְטֹרֶת
**Transliteration:** qᵉṭôreth (ket-o'-reth)
**Part of Speech:** Feminine noun
**BLB Count:** 60

### Definition
From H6999; a fumigation: (sweet) incense, perfume.

### Translations
- "incense" — 27x (37.5%)
- "of incense" — 19x (26.4%)
- "the incense" — 5x
- "a perfume" / "the perfume" — 2x total

### Leviticus 16 Occurrences
- 16:12 — "sweet incense beaten small" (qetoreth sammim daqqah)
- 16:13 — "the incense" (ha-qetoreth, 2x) — placed on fire, cloud covers mercy seat

### Key Notes
The incense (qetoreth) in the DOA is beaten fine (daqqah) and mixed with spices (sammim). The incense cloud serves a PROTECTIVE function: "that the cloud of the incense may cover the mercy seat that [is] upon the testimony, that he die not" (v.13). The same word appears in Nadab and Abihu's unauthorized offering (LEV 10:1). The incense symbolizes prayer (PSA 141:2; REV 5:8; 8:3,4). The smoke filling the temple in REV 15:8 parallels the DOA incense cloud.
