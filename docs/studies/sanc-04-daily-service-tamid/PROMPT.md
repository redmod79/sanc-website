**Key Findings from Related Studies:**

**From sanc-03-sanctuary-furniture/CONCLUSION.md:**
- The "Perpetual Principle" (Section XII): Every piece of Holy Place furniture is associated with perpetual, uninterrupted maintenance: lamp burns "always" (Exo 27:20), showbread "before me alway" (Exo 25:30), incense "perpetual" (Exo 30:8), sacred fire never goes out (Lev 6:13)
- Christ fulfills this perpetuity: "ever liveth to make intercession" (Heb 7:25), "the living bread" (John 6:51), light "of the world" (John 8:12), the Lamb's light never goes out (Rev 21:23; 22:5)
- Incense explicitly identified as prayer: Psa 141:2 (OT); Rev 5:8 and 8:3-4 (NT equative: thymiamaton "are" the prayers of the saints)
- The salvation sequence: altar (death) -> laver (cleansing) -> lampstand (light) -> showbread (sustenance) -> incense (prayer) -> mercy seat (mercy) — confirmed by Heb 10:19-22
- The incense altar occupies a liminal position: physically in Holy Place but oriented "before the mercy seat" (Exo 30:6) — prayer reaches through the veil
- kapporeth/hilasterion bridge (H3727/G2435): the mercy seat IS where God meets humanity; Christ IS the mercy seat (Rom 3:25)

**From daniel-8-9-sanctuary-day-of-atonement/CONCLUSION.md:**

- The evening-morning terminology in Daniel 8:14 matches Day of Atonement language (singular erev, evening-first order), NOT daily sacrifice language (dual arbayim, morning-first order)
- Daniel 9:24 uses Day of Atonement vocabulary: kaphar (make reconciliation/atonement)
- The 2300 evening-mornings concern the sanctuary being "vindicated" (tsadaq) — this vindicates the entire system including the tamid
- Daniel's prayer mirrors Day of Atonement observance: fasting, confession, afflicting soul
- Key distinction: the daily (tamid) represents REGULAR ministry; the little horn's attack on it represents an assault on Christ's continuous intercession
