# Topics Research

## Question
What was the daily (tamid) service in the sanctuary, and what does it teach about God's ongoing provision and Christ's continuous intercession? How does the morning and evening sacrifice establish the rhythm that the annual Day of Atonement interrupts?

## Related Topics Discovered
- The **morning-evening pattern** appears across ALL Holy Place activities: lamp, incense, showbread (weekly but before the Lord continually), and the burnt offering itself.
- The **Nave's PRIEST** entry connects ALL daily duties to the priestly role, confirming the daily service as the defining activity of the priesthood.
- The **BLOOD** entry traces blood destination differences: sin offering blood → horns of altar + before veil; burnt offering blood → sprinkled on/around the altar; Day of Atonement blood → mercy seat. This progression of blood access corresponds to deepening levels of atonement.
- The **ATONEMENT** entry distinguishes daily individual atonement (Lev 4) from annual corporate atonement (Lev 16), with the daily laying the groundwork that the annual resolves.
