## G5357 phiale (bowl/vial)
12 occ, ALL in Revelation. "broad shallow cup"
Rev 5:8, 15:7, 16:1,2,3,4,8,10,12,17, 17:1, 21:9
Critical: 5:8 gemousas thymiamaton (full of incense=prayers) -> 15:7 gemousas tou thymou (full of wrath)

## G2586 kapnos (smoke)
13 occ (12 Rev, 1 Acts). "smoke"
Sacred: 8:4 (ascending with prayers before God)
Theophanic: 15:8 (from God's glory, filling temple)
Destructive: 9:2,3,17,18 (pit/horses); 14:11 (torment); 18:9,18; 19:3 (Babylon)
Direction: 8:4 anebe (ascended, UP) vs 15:8 egemisthe (was filled, OUT from God)

## G3485 naos (inner shrine/temple)

Rev: 3:12; 7:15; 11:1,2,19; 14:15,17; 15:5,6,8(x2); 16:1,17; 21:22(x2)
Peak: 6x in Rev 15-16. Terminus: 21:22 (denied then predicated)

## G2379 thysiastErion (altar)

Rev 6:9 (unqualified), 8:3 (chrysoun), 8:5, 9:13 (chrysoun), 11:1, 14:18, 16:7 (speaks)

## G2368 thymiama (incense)
6 occ (4 Rev). From thymiaomai (to burn incense). Aroma/fragrant powder
Rev 5:8 (=prayers, explicit identification), 8:3 (offered with prayers), 8:4 (ascending), 18:13 (desacralized)

## G2372 thymos (wrath/fury)
18 occ (10 Rev). From thyomai (to breathe hard). Passion/fury
Rev 12:12; 14:8,10,19; 15:1,7; 16:1,19; 18:3; 19:15
Shares root thym- with thymiama. 15:7 replaces thymiama in identical construction.

## G3031 libanotos (censer)
2 occ (BOTH Rev 8:3,5). From libanos (frankincense). Censer for burning incense.
LXX: 1 Chr 9:29. Both NT uses show the intercession->judgment transformation.

## G3087 lychnia (lampstand)
7 occ (ALL Rev). Candlestick/lampstand. = churches (1:20).
Rev 1:12,13,20; 2:1,5; 11:4. HP furniture (Heb 9:2).

## G2985 lampas (torch/lamp)
9 occ (1 Rev at 4:5). DIFFERENT word from lychnia.
Rev 4:5: lampades pyros = seven Spirits of God. Throne room light.

## G3088 lychnos (lamp/light)
14 occ (3 Rev). Rev 18:23 (Babylon's light extinguished), 21:23 (Lamb IS the lychnos), 22:5 (no need of lychnos)
Final term in light-word triad: lychnia -> lampas -> lychnos negated.

## G2787 kibotos (ark)
6 occ (1 Rev at 11:19). Ark of the covenant = MHP marker.
Both verbs at 11:19 are divine passives (enoige, Ophthe).

## G4637 skenoO (to tabernacle/dwell)

## G5055 teleo (to complete/fulfill)
Rev 15:1 etelesthe, 15:8 telesthOsin, 20:3 telesthe.

## G2223 zone (girdle/belt)

## G1072 gemizo (to fill)
Rev 8:5 egemisen (Aor Act, he filled the censer with fire)
Rev 15:8 egemisthe (Aor Pass, the naos was filled with smoke)
Same root for both vessel-filling actions; one active (agent fills censer), one passive (naos is filled by divine glory).
