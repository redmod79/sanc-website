KEY OBSERVATIONS:
1. hamartian epoiesen (ἁμαρτίαν ἐποίησεν) = "[God] MADE [him] sin." This is the NT equivalent of the chattat double meaning -- the sinless one becomes identified with sin itself.
2. The structure is chiastic: sin (unknown) --> sin (made) --> righteousness (become). The exchange is complete: our sin placed on him, his righteousness placed on us.

4. hina genometha (ἵνα γενώμεθα) = purpose clause with Aorist Subjunctive -- "in order that we might become" -- the purpose of the sin-transfer is our transformation into God's righteousness.
