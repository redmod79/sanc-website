**From sanc-01-why-a-sanctuary/CONCLUSION.md:**
- The sanctuary's stated purpose is divine dwelling: "that I may dwell among them" (EXO 25:8). The Hebrew verb shakan (H7931) denotes permanent, settled dwelling.
- Dual naming: miqdash (H4720, from qadash "to be holy") names it by character; mishkan (H4908, from shakan "to dwell") names it by purpose. Together they pose the central question: how can a holy God dwell among unholy people?
- Eden as proto-sanctuary is established through east-facing entrance (GEN 3:24 / EXO 27:13-14), cherubim guardians, precious materials, priestly vocabulary (le'avdah uleshomrah), and the tree of life.
- The graduated holiness zones (courtyard -> holy place -> most holy place) control access to the increasingly concentrated divine presence.
- Heavenly pattern: EXO 25:9,40; ACT 7:44; HEB 8:5; 9:23-24 -- Moses received a pattern (tabnith) from a heavenly original.
- Glory-filling pattern ratifies each stage: EXO 40:34-35; 1KI 8:10-11; 2CH 7:1; REV 15:8.
- The linguistic thread shakan -> mishkan -> Shekinah -> skenoo (G4637) bridges both testaments.
- Open question identified: "How each specific element of the sanctuary furniture teaches the dwelling theology" -- directly addressed by this study.

**From revs-47-sanctuary-temple-typology/CONCLUSION.md:**
