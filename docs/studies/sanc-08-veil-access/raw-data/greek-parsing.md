**Key observations:**
- "hen" (which) = relative pronoun, accusative singular FEMININE — refers back to "eisodon" (entrance, feminine) in v.19
- "enekainisen" (he inaugurated/dedicated) = AORIST active indicative — a completed past action
- "hodon" (way) = accusative — in apposition to "hen"
- "prosphaton" (new/fresh) = accusative, modifying "hodon"
- "zosan" (living) = present active PARTICIPLE — the way is ongoing/alive
- "dia tou katapetasmatos" (through the veil) = genitive with dia — passage THROUGH
- "tout' estin" (that is) = explanatory clause — THIS IS the interpretive crux
- "tes sarkos autou" (of his flesh) = genitive singular FEMININE

The genitive "tes sarkos" could modify either "katapetasmatos" (identifying flesh = veil) or "hodon" (the way through his flesh). The syntactic ambiguity is deliberate.
