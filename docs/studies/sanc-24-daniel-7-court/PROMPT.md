### Focus Areas

1. **Daniel 7:9-14 as Heavenly Court Scene**: The Aramaic text of Dan 7:9-14 describes a court convening with precise legal/judicial vocabulary. The research agent should retrieve FULL Daniel 7 chapter text, run hebrew_parser.py on Dan 7:9-14 (noting that Daniel 7 is Aramaic, not Hebrew — check if parser handles it), and look up the Aramaic terms: diyn (H1780), yetib ("took its seat"), karse ("thrones"), siphrin ("books"). The key finding from hist-02 that the Son of Man comes TO the Ancient of Days (not to earth) needs Aramaic preposition analysis.

2. **Dan 7:9-10 Imagery and DOA Parallels**: Dan 7:9's white garments/hair and fire imagery parallel Day of Atonement elements: the high priest's white linen garments (Lev 16:4, H906 bad), the fire on the altar, the cloud of incense (Lev 16:12-13). The research agent should retrieve Lev 16:1-34 full chapter, compare vocabulary: fire (H784 esh / Aramaic nuwra), white (H3836 laban), garment. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on DAN 7:9 and DAN 7:10 in both directions.

3. **Dan 7:13-14 — The Son of Man "Coming" to the Ancient of Days**: This is NOT the second coming but a heavenly approach. The direction is toward God, not toward earth. The research agent should run cross-testament parallels on DAN 7:13 and DAN 7:14 (both directions), retrieve Psalm 110:1 for "sit at my right hand" parallel, and compare with Matt 26:64 where Jesus fuses Ps 110:1 + Dan 7:13.

4. **Dan 7:22 — "Judgment Given to the Saints"**: The verdict is rendered IN FAVOR OF the saints. This is forensic language (cf. tsadaq in Dan 8:14). The research agent should look up H1780 (diyn, Aramaic judgment), retrieve the context of Dan 7:18-27 (the angelic interpretation), and run cross-testament parallels on DAN 7:22.

5. **Revelation's Distribution of Daniel 7 Imagery**: Rev distributes Dan 7 across its structure: Rev 4-5 (throne/court), Rev 13 (composite beast), Rev 14:14 (Son of Man on cloud), Rev 20 (books opened, judgment). The research agent should retrieve full chapters Rev 4, Rev 5, Rev 20, and run greek_parser.py on Rev 4:2-4, Rev 5:11-12, Rev 20:11-12. Also run cross-testament parallels on these passages.

6. **The Books Motif: Dan 7:10 → Rev 5:1 → Rev 20:12**: Books are opened in Dan 7:10 as part of the judgment. Rev 5:1 has a sealed scroll (biblion). Rev 20:12 has books (biblia) opened for judgment. The research agent should trace this motif: look up G976 (biblion), G975 (biblos), and compare the three contexts.

7. **Day of Atonement Structural Parallels**: The DOA (Lev 16) features: (a) high priest enters MHP alone, (b) cloud of incense, (c) blood sprinkled, (d) no one else in tabernacle (Lev 16:17), (e) judgment of sin records, (f) scapegoat. Dan 7's judgment scene parallels: (a) the Ancient of Days "sits" (entering into judgment), (b) fire stream (cf. cloud/fire theophany), (c) books opened (cf. sin records), (d) verdict rendered. The research agent should retrieve Heb 9:1-28 full chapter for the shadow-substance framework.

8. **Rev 4-5 as the Dan 7:9-14 Parallel**: Rev 4-5 throne scene parallels Dan 7:9-14 in specific details: throne (Dan 7:9/Rev 4:2), fire (Dan 7:9-10/Rev 4:5), thousands of angels (Dan 7:10/Rev 5:11), the Lamb approaches the throne and receives the scroll (Dan 7:13-14/Rev 5:7), universal worship (Dan 7:14/Rev 5:9-14). The research agent should run detailed comparison and greek_parser.py on Rev 4:2-5, Rev 5:6-7, Rev 5:11-14.

9. **The Day-Year Principle in Daniel 7**: Dan 7:25's "time, times, and half a time" = 3.5 prophetic years = 1260 prophetic days. The day-year principle (Num 14:34, Eze 4:6) converts this to 1260 literal years. The research agent should retrieve Num 14:34 and Eze 4:6, and note the mathematical equivalence: 3.5 times = 42 months = 1260 days (Rev 11:2-3, 12:6,14, 13:5).

10. **Rev 20 White Throne Judgment and Dan 7**: Rev 20:11-12 (great white throne, books opened, dead judged) echoes Dan 7:9-10 (thrones, books opened, judgment set). But Rev 20 is post-millennial while Dan 7 appears pre-millennial. The research agent should retrieve Rev 20:1-15 full text, run greek_parser.py on Rev 20:11-12, and run cross-testament parallels on REV 20:11 and REV 20:12.
