### Focus Areas

1. **Inauguration Phase — Biblical Evidence and Boundaries**: The prior ascension study established that Christ entered the MHP as an inaugural act (Heb 9:18, 10:20; enkainizo G1457). But what marks the END of the inauguration phase and the BEGINNING of ongoing intercession? The research agent should retrieve full chapters Heb 7-10, Acts 2, Exo 40, and parse Heb 9:18 and 10:20 (Greek), and look up G1457 (enkainizo) and G4274 (prodromos). Also retrieve Dan 9:24 and parse the Hebrew for mashach qodesh qodashim.

2. **Intercession Phase — Nature and Duration**: Heb 7:25 says Christ "ever liveth to make intercession." What does this ongoing ministry look like? How does it correspond to the daily tamid service (Exo 30:7-8; Lev 6:12-13; 24:3-9)? The research agent should retrieve full chapters Rev 5, Rev 8, and run parallels on Heb 7:25, 8:1-2, 1 John 2:1, Rom 8:34, and Rev 8:3-4. Look up G1793 (entygchano — to intercede), G3875 (parakletos — advocate), and G3316 (mesites — mediator).

3. **Transition from Intercession to Judgment**: What marks the transition from Phase 2 to Phase 3? Rev 11:19 reveals the ark; Rev 14:7 declares "the hour of judgment IS COME." The prior Rev 15:8 study showed the vessel transformation arc (bowls of prayer → bowls of wrath). The research agent should retrieve full chapters Rev 11, 14, 15, and run parallels on Rev 11:19, Rev 14:6-7, Rev 15:5-8. Parse Rev 14:7 Greek for "the hour of his judgment is come" (aorist elthen).

4. **Day of Atonement Phase — Judgment in Revelation**: How does the DOA typology map onto Christ's judgment ministry? The earthly DOA had specific elements: high priest enters MHP alone (Lev 16:17), blood sprinkled on mercy seat (Lev 16:14-15), scapegoat bears iniquity away (Lev 16:20-22). The research agent should retrieve full chapter Lev 16, full chapter Rev 20, and run parallels on Dan 7:9-10, Dan 8:14, Rev 15:8. Look up H3722 (kaphar — to atone) and H2891 (taher — to cleanse).

5. **Phase 4 — Emergence and Second Coming**: Lev 16:23-24 records the high priest changing garments and emerging; Lev 9:22-24 shows Aaron emerging to bless and fire falling. Heb 9:28 says Christ shall "appear the second time without sin unto salvation." Does this constitutes a distinct fourth phase? The research agent should retrieve Lev 16:23-28, Lev 9:22-24, Heb 9:28, Rev 19:11-16, Rev 22:12, and Num 6:22-27. Parse Heb 9:28 Greek (ophthesetai — future passive of horao). Run parallels on Heb 9:28.

6. **The Sanctuary Vocabulary Across Phases**: Different vocabulary marks each phase. Inauguration = enkainizo (G1457), mashach (H4886), qadash (H6942). Intercession = entygchano (G1793), parakletos (G3875). Judgment = krisis (G2920), katharizo (G2511), tsadaq (H6663). The research agent should look up all these Strong's numbers and trace how they distribute across the three/four phases.

7. **Revelation's Sanctuary Progression**: Revelation seems to move through sanctuary phases sequentially: Rev 1:12-13 (lampstands/HP), Rev 4-5 (throne room/inauguration), Rev 5:8/8:3-4 (incense/intercession), Rev 11:19 (ark revealed/transition), Rev 15:5-8 (glory fills/DOA), Rev 19-22 (emergence/new creation). Retrieve Rev 1:12-20 full context, and run parallels on Rev 1:12-13 and Rev 4:5. Look up G3087 (lychnia — lampstand) and G2379 (thysiasterion — altar).

8. **Hebrews' Temporal Language**: Hebrews places some events in the past (sat down, Heb 1:3; entered once, 9:12), some in the present (ever liveth to intercede, 7:25; minister of the sanctuary, 8:2), and some in the future (appear second time, 9:28; judgment, 10:27). This temporal structure may map onto the three/four phases. The research agent should collect all temporal markers in Heb 1-10 and parse key verbs for tense.
