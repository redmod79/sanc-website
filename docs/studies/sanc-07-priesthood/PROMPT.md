- DOA blood ministry moves innermost to outermost, reversing the year-long inward accumulation
- Christ is hilasterion (mercy seat, Rom 3:25) — where blood met God's presence

### Focus Areas

1. **Priestly Qualifications — Levitical vs. Melchizedek**: Tool discoveries show the PRIEST entry distinguishes pre-Mosaic priesthood (Melchizedek, GEN 14:18) from the Mosaic institution (EXO 28:1-4; NUM 3:10). The qualification requirements include Levite descent (NUM 3:5-10; 18:1-7), physical perfection (LEV 21:17-23), holiness (LEV 21:6,7; 22:9,16), and consecration (EXO 29:1-9,19-35; LEV 8:6-35). The research agent should retrieve all these passages with context and investigate how Christ fulfills these qualifications despite NOT being of Levite descent — Heb 7:13-14 explicitly acknowledges Christ is from Judah. The Melchizedek order (PSA 110:4; HEB 7:1-21) is the key to resolving this. Run cross-testament parallels on PSA 110:4.

2. **Consecration Ceremony — "Filling the Hands"**: The Strong's search revealed H4394 millu ("filling," i.e., consecration) as the technical term for priestly ordination. Exo 29:1-37 and Lev 8 describe the seven-day consecration ceremony. The research agent should retrieve EXO 29:1-37 and LEV 8 with full chapter context, parse the Hebrew of the key consecration verbs (qadash H6942, millu H4394), and trace how Christ's consecration differs (Heb 5:5-6; 7:28). Also retrieve Lev 8:7-9 for the vesting ceremony.

3. **Regular Priest Garments vs. High Priest Garments**: Tool discoveries show the PRIEST entry lists vestments at EXO 28:2-43; 39:1-29, with the BREASTPLATE entry at EXO 28:15-30 and EPHOD at EXO 28:6-14,31-35. The regular priests wore white linen (EXO 28:40-43; LEV 6:10), while the high priest had the full regalia: breastplate, ephod, robe, mitre with "HOLINESS TO THE LORD" plate (EXO 28:36; 39:30). The research agent should retrieve the FULL TEXT of EXO 28 (all 43 verses) and EXO 39:1-31. Investigate each garment's symbolic significance. Particularly important: the high priest carries Israel on his shoulders (onyx stones, EXO 28:9-12) and on his heart (breastplate stones, EXO 28:29) — two modes of bearing the nation.

4. **The Breastplate, Urim and Thummim, and Divine Guidance**: The URIM AND THUMMIM entry shows these were in the breastplate (EXO 28:30; LEV 8:8) and used for divine guidance (NUM 27:21; 1SA 23:9,12; 28:6). Investigate how Christ fulfills the Urim and Thummim function — does the NT present Christ as the source of divine guidance and light? Check JHN 1:9; 14:6; COL 2:3.

5. **High Priest's DOA Garment Change — Glory to Linen to Glory**: The DOA involves a remarkable garment change: the high priest normally wore the golden glory garments but changed into plain white linen to enter the Most Holy Place (LEV 16:4), then changed back to the glory garments afterward (LEV 16:23-24). The research agent should retrieve LEV 16:1-24 with full context, parse the Hebrew for the linen garments (bad H906), and investigate the Christological significance. Does this map to Christ's incarnation (glory to humility) and subsequent glorification? Compare with PHP 2:5-11 (kenosis) and JHN 17:5 (return to glory). Run cross-testament parallels on LEV 16:4 and PHP 2:7.
