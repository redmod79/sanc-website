  - **Heb 8:2** -- Christ as "A minister (leitourgos) of the sanctuary and true tabernacle"

## G39 - ἅγιον (hagion) - "holy place/sanctuary"
- Part of Speech: adjective (neuter substantive)
- BLB Count: 11 (as neuter substantive referring to sanctuary)
- EVERY Hebrews occurrence as sanctuary reference:
  - **Heb 8:2** -- ton hagion = "sanctuary" (Gen Pl N) -- Christ ministers of THE sanctuary
  - **Heb 9:1** -- to hagion kosmikon = "worldly sanctuary" (Acc Sg N) -- first covenant had
  - **Heb 9:2** -- hagia = "the sanctuary" (KJV) -- the first compartment
  - **Heb 9:3** -- hagia hagion = "the Holiest of all" -- the Most Holy Place (Gen Pl N)
  - **Heb 9:8** -- ton hagion = "holiest of all" (Gen Pl N) -- way into was not yet manifest
  - **Heb 9:12** -- ta hagia = "the holy place" (Acc Pl N) -- Christ entered into
  - **Heb 9:24** -- hagia = "holy places" (Acc Pl N) -- not made with hands
  - **Heb 9:25** -- ta hagia = "the holy place" (Acc Pl N) -- high priest enters yearly
  - **Heb 10:19** -- ton hagion = "the holiest" (Gen Pl N) -- boldness to enter
  - **Heb 13:11** -- ta hagia = "the sanctuary" (Acc Pl N) -- blood brought into by HP

NOTE: In Hebrews, the same form (ta hagia / ton hagion = neuter plural of hagios) is used for BOTH the general sanctuary AND specific compartments. Context must determine reference.

## G1457 Hebrew Source (from --hebrew-source)
- Primary source: **H2596 (chanak)** -- "to dedicate, inaugurate, train up" (PMI: 11.30)
  - This is the root of "Hanukkah" (Feast of Dedication, Jhn 10:22)
  - Used for dedicating houses (Deut 20:5), the temple (1 Ki 8:63), the altar (2 Chr 7:9)
- Secondary: **H2318 (chadash)** -- "to be new, renew" (PMI: 9.56)
  - Used for renewing the kingdom (1 Sam 11:14), renewing the altar (2 Chr 15:8)
- The combination of chanak and chadash in the LXX background confirms enkainizo carries both inauguration/dedication AND renewal/making-new semantics
