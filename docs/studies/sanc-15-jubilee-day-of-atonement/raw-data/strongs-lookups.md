## H1350 — גָּאַל (ga'al) — redeem (kinsman-redeemer)
- Transliteration: gâʼal (gaw-al)
- POS: verb
- BLB Count: 104
- Definition: a primitive root, to redeem (according to the Oriental law of kinship), i.e. to be the next of kin (and as such to buy back a relative's property, marry his widow, etc.)
- KJV: redeemed, revenger, avenger, kinsman, redeemer, deliver, ransom, purchase, redeem
- Wide semantic range: kinsman-redeemer (Ruth), blood avenger (Numbers), divine redeemer (Isaiah)
- Key clusters: Leviticus 25 (15x — property redemption), Ruth (13x — kinsman-redeemer), Isaiah (22x — divine redeemer), Psalms (12x — personal redemption)

## H6299 — פָּדָה (padah) — ransom/redeem
- Transliteration: pâdâh (paw-daw)
- POS: verb
- BLB Count: 59
- Definition: a primitive root; to sever, i.e. ransom; generally to release, preserve
- KJV: redeemed, redeem, deliver, ransom, rescue
