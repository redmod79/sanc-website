    Raw Strong's Lookup Output¶

    H1865 — דְּרוֹר (deror) — liberty/release¶

    • Transliteration: dᵉrôwr (der-ore)
    • POS: masculine noun
    • BLB Count: 8 occurrences
    • Definition: from an unused root (meaning to move rapidly); freedom; hence, spontaneity of outflow, and so clear
    • KJV translations: "liberty" (5x), "pure" (1x), "a liberty" (1x), "the" (1x), "of liberty" (1x) — 9 total translation instances

    Occurrences:¶

    • Exo 30:23 — "pure" (pure myrrh — different sense)
    • Lev 25:10 — "liberty" (proclaim liberty throughout the land)
    • Isa 61:1 — "liberty" (proclaim liberty to the captives)
    • Jer 34:8 — "liberty" (Zedekiah proclaims liberty)
    • Jer 34:15 — "liberty" (proclaiming liberty every man to his neighbour)
    • Jer 34:17 — "liberty" (2x: you did not proclaim liberty... I proclaim liberty to the sword)
    • Ezk 46:17 — "of liberty" (the year of liberty)

    LXX Mapping (Top result):¶

    • G859 ἄφεσις (aphesis) — count: 6, PMI: 8.96
    • Definition: freedom; (figuratively) pardon: deliverance, forgiveness, liberty, remission
    • NT occurrences: Matt 26:28; Mark 1:4; 3:29; Luke 1:77; 3:3; 4:18; 4:19; 24:47; Acts 2:38; 5:31; 10:43; 13:38; 26:18; Eph 1:7; Col 1:14; Heb 9:22; 10:18
    • Key finding: The LXX translates Hebrew deror (Jubilee liberty) as aphesis — the same word used in NT for "forgiveness of sins"

    H3104 — יוֹבֵל (yobel) — jubilee/ram's horn¶

    • Transliteration: yôwbêl (yo-bale)
    • POS: masculine noun
    • BLB Count: 27 occurrences
    • Definition: the blast of a horn (from its continuous sound); specifically, the signal of the silver trumpets; hence, the instrument itself and the festival thus introduced
    • KJV translations: "jubile" (6x), "the jubile" (4x), "of the jubile" (3x), "in the jubile" (4x), "of rams' horns" (4x), "the trumpet" (1x), others

    Distribution:¶

    • Exo 19:13 — ram's horn at Sinai
    • Lev 25 — 15 occurrences (the Jubilee legislation)
    • Lev 27 — 5 occurrences (valuation tied to Jubilee)
    • Num 36:4 — inheritance at Jubilee
    • Joshua 6:4,5,6,8,13 — 7 occurrences (rams' horns at Jericho)

    LXX Mapping:¶

    • G859 ἄφεσις (aphesis) — count: 18, PMI: 8.77 (TOP RESULT)
    • G1763 ἐνιαυτός (eniautos = "year") — count: 10
    • G4536 σάλπιγξ (salpinx = "trumpet") — count: 4
    • G3084 λυτρόω (lytroo = "redeem") — count: 3
    • Key finding: Both yobel (H3104) AND deror (H1865) map primarily to G859 aphesis — the Jubilee IS forgiveness/release in the LXX

    H7782 — שׁוֹפָר (shophar) — trumpet/cornet¶

    • Transliteration: shôwphâr (sho-far)
    • POS: masculine noun
    • BLB Count: 72 occurrences
    • Definition: from the original sense of incising; a cornet (as giving a clear sound) or curved horn
    • KJV: "trumpet" (most common), "cornet" (some)
    • Key occurrences: Exo 19:16,19; 20:18 (Sinai); Lev 25:9 (Jubilee on DOA); Josh 6 (Jericho); Isa 27:13; Jer 4:5; Joel 2:1,15; Zech 9:14

    G629 — ἀπολύτρωσις (apolytrosis) — redemption¶

    • Transliteration: apolýtrōsis
    • POS: feminine noun
    • BLB Count: 10
    • Definition: (the act) ransom in full
    • KJV: "redemption" (6x), "the redemption" (2x), "of redemption" (1x), "deliverance" (1x)

    Verses:¶

    • Luke 21:28 — "your redemption draweth nigh"
    • Rom 3:24 — "through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus"
    • Rom 8:23 — "the redemption of our body"
    • 1 Cor 1:30 — "who of God is made unto us... redemption"
    • Eph 1:7 — "we have redemption through his blood"
    • Eph 1:14 — "the redemption of the purchased possession"
    • Eph 4:30 — "sealed unto the day of redemption"
    • Col 1:14 — "we have redemption through his blood"
    • Heb 9:15 — "for the redemption of transgressions"
    • Heb 11:35 — "not accepting deliverance"

    G3084 — λυτρόω (lytroo) — to redeem¶

    • Transliteration: lytróō
    • POS: verb
    • BLB Count: 3 NT occurrences
    • Definition: to ransom (literally or figuratively)
    • Occurrences: Luke 24:21 ("we trusted that it had been he which should have redeemed Israel"), Titus 2:14 ("who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us"), 1 Peter 1:18 ("ye were not redeemed with corruptible things")

    G2817 — κληρονομία (kleronomia) — inheritance¶

    • Transliteration: klēronomía
    • POS: feminine noun
    • BLB Count: 14
    • KJV: "inheritance" (10x), "an inheritance" (3x), "any inheritance" (1x)

    Verses:¶

    • Matt 21:38 — "let us seize on his inheritance"
    • Mark 12:7 — "the inheritance shall be ours"
    • Luke 12:13 — "divide the inheritance with me"
    • Luke 20:14 — "that the inheritance may be ours"
    • Acts 7:5 — "he gave him none inheritance in it"
    • Acts 20:32 — "give you an inheritance among all them which are sanctified"
    • Gal 3:18 — "if the inheritance be of the law"
    • Eph 1:14 — "the earnest of our inheritance"
    • Eph 1:18 — "the riches of the glory of his inheritance"
    • Eph 5:5 — "hath any inheritance in the kingdom"
    • Col 3:24 — "ye shall receive the reward of the inheritance"
    • Heb 9:15 — "receive the promise of eternal inheritance"
    • Heb 11:8 — "which he should after receive for an inheritance"
    • 1 Pet 1:4 — (not listed in tool but related)

    H1350 — גָּאַל (ga'al) — redeem (kinsman-redeemer)¶

    • Transliteration: gâʼal (gaw-al)
    • POS: verb
    • BLB Count: 104
    • Definition: a primitive root, to redeem (according to the Oriental law of kinship), i.e. to be the next of kin (and as such to buy back a relative's property, marry his widow, etc.)
    • KJV: redeemed, revenger, avenger, kinsman, redeemer, deliver, ransom, purchase, redeem
    • Wide semantic range: kinsman-redeemer (Ruth), blood avenger (Numbers), divine redeemer (Isaiah)
    • Key clusters: Leviticus 25 (15x — property redemption), Ruth (13x — kinsman-redeemer), Isaiah (22x — divine redeemer), Psalms (12x — personal redemption)

    H6299 — פָּדָה (padah) — ransom/redeem¶

    • Transliteration: pâdâh (paw-daw)
    • POS: verb
    • BLB Count: 59
    • Definition: a primitive root; to sever, i.e. ransom; generally to release, preserve
    • KJV: redeemed, redeem, deliver, ransom, rescue
    • Key distinction from ga'al: padah = commercial ransom (price-based), ga'al = kinship redemption (relationship-based)
    • Key passages: Exo 13:13,15 (firstborn); Deut 7:8; 9:26 (Exodus); Psa 49:8 ("none can redeem his brother"); Isa 35:10; 51:11 (ransomed of the LORD); Hosea 13:14 ("I will ransom them from the power of the grave")

    G1657 — ἐλευθερία (eleutheria) — liberty/freedom¶

    • Transliteration: eleuthería
    • POS: feminine noun
    • BLB Count: 11
    • KJV: "liberty" (9x, 100% of translation instances)
    • Occurrences: Rom 8:21; 1 Cor 10:29; 2 Cor 3:17; Gal 2:4; 5:1; 5:13(2x); Jas 1:25; 2:12; 1 Pet 2:16; 2 Pet 2:19

    G605 — ἀποκατάστασις (apokatastasis) — restitution/restoration¶

    • Transliteration: apokatástasis
    • POS: feminine noun
    • BLB Count: 1 (hapax legomenon)
    • Definition: reconstitution
    • KJV: "of restitution" (1x)
    • Only occurrence: Acts 3:21 — "the times of restitution of all things"
    • From ἀπό + κατά + ἵστημι = "restore to the original/proper state"

    G859 — ἄφεσις (aphesis) — release/forgiveness¶

    (Discovered through LXX mapping — key bridge term) - Transliteration: áphesis - POS: feminine noun - BLB Count: 17 - Definition: freedom; (figuratively) pardon: deliverance, forgiveness, liberty, remission - Hebrew sources: H3104 yobel (count 18), H1865 deror (count 6), H8059 shemittah (count 4) - Critical discovery: aphesis bridges the Jubilee vocabulary (deror, yobel) to NT "forgiveness of sins" - NT occurrences include: Isa 61:1 (LXX); Matt 26:28; Mark 1:4; Luke 1:77; 3:3; 4:18,19; 24:47; Acts 2:38; 5:31; 10:43; 13:38; 26:18; Eph 1:7; Col 1:14; Heb 9:22; 10:18
