    Focus Areas¶

    1. The Jubilee Institution (Lev 25:8-55): What exactly does the Jubilee accomplish? Tool discoveries show the Jubilee has three distinct functions: (a) liberty/emancipation of servants (Lev 25:10,39-42,54), (b) return to ancestral land/possession (Lev 25:10,13,28), and (c) economic reset/debt cancellation. The research agent should retrieve LEV 25:8-55 with full chapter context, parse the Hebrew of key terms (deror H1865, yobel H3104, shophar H7782), and run hebrew_parser.py on LEV 25:9-10 to examine the grammar of the proclamation command.

    2. The Timing Nexus: WHY does the Jubilee trumpet sound ON the Day of Atonement (Lev 25:9)? The central theological question of this study. Nave's shows the Jubilee is proclaimed on Tishri 10 — the very day judgment/cleansing occurs (Lev 16:30). The research agent should retrieve LEV 25:9, LEV 23:27-32, and LEV 16:30 together, noting the deliberate calendrical overlap. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot and --hybrid-nt on LEV 25:9. Also retrieve NUM 29:7 for the DOA date reference.

    3. Deror (H1865) — The "Liberty" of Jubilee traced through Scripture: Semantic Strong's identified H1865 (deror) as the core liberty term with only 8 OT occurrences. Nave's LIBERTY entry links it to LEV 25:10, JER 34:8,15-17, ISA 61:1, and ISA 63:4. The research agent must run search_strongs.py --lookup H1865 and --verses H1865 to find ALL occurrences, trace how deror is used in Jer 34 (Zedekiah's failed liberty proclamation) and Isa 61:1 (messianic proclamation). Also run --lxx-map H1865 to find the Greek equivalent.

    4. Isaiah 61:1-2 and Luke 4:16-21 — Jesus Claims to Fulfill the Jubilee: Nave's cross-references ISA 61:1-2 with LUK 4:18-19. Jesus reads the Jubilee passage and declares "This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears" (Luk 4:21). The research agent should retrieve ISA 61:1-7 and LUK 4:16-30 with full chapter context, note that Jesus STOPS READING mid-verse at Isa 61:2 (omitting "the day of vengeance"), and run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on both ISA 61:1 and LUK 4:18. Also run greek_parser.py on LUK 4:18-21 to examine the Greek of "fulfilled" (pleroo).

    5. Jeremiah 34:8-22 — The Anti-Jubilee: What happens when liberty is proclaimed but revoked? Tool discoveries found JER 34:8,15-17 under both LIBERTY and JUBILEE. Zedekiah proclaims deror (liberty) but the people revoke it, re-enslaving their brothers. God responds: "I proclaim a liberty FOR YOU — to the sword, to the pestilence, and to the famine" (34:17). The research agent should retrieve JER 34:8-22 with full chapter context and examine the ironic reversal of Jubilee language. Run hebrew_parser.py on JER 34:15-17 to trace the deror usage.

    6. John 8:32-36 — Freedom, Bondage, and the Son: Nave's LIBERTY entry includes JHN 8:32,33,36. Jesus says "the truth shall make you free" and "if the Son shall make you free, ye shall be free indeed" — Jubilee liberty language applied to spiritual freedom from sin. The research agent should retrieve JHN 8:31-36 with chapter context and run greek_parser.py on JHN 8:32-36 to examine the Greek freedom vocabulary (eleutheroo G1659).

    7. The Seventh Trumpet as Eschatological Jubilee (Rev 11:15-18): Sanc-14 identified the seventh trumpet as potentially the eschatological Jubilee trumpet. Rev 11:15 — "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord" — is kingdom-restoration language (inheritance returning to rightful owner). Rev 11:18 combines judgment ("thy wrath is come, the time of the dead that they should be judged") with reward ("give reward unto thy servants"). The research agent should retrieve REV 11:15-19 with full chapter context, run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot and --hybrid-nt on REV 11:15, and run greek_parser.py on REV 11:15.

    8. Revelation 18:13 and 21:1-7 — Babylon's Anti-Jubilee vs. the Ultimate Jubilee: Babylon's merchandise list ends with "slaves and souls of men" (Rev 18:13) — the opposite of Jubilee liberation.
