    From law-18-hebrews-8-10/CONCLUSION.md: - Heb 8-10 is a single sustained argument: Christ is superior high priest (8:1-6), first covenant's "fault" was "with them" (autous, masc. pl. = the people, 8:8), God's laws written on hearts (8:10; 10:16) - The "shadow" = the sacrificial/ceremonial system (Heb 8:5; 9:9; 10:1) - "Carnal ordinances" = regulations about "meats, drinks, washings" (9:10) - What is "taken away" = "sacrifice and offering and burnt offerings" (10:5-9) - What CONTINUES = "my laws" (nomous mou) on hearts (8:10; 10:16), Christ's priesthood in heavenly sanctuary (8:1-2; 9:11-12,24) - Key Greek: archiereus in Heb 8:1 = Christ as high priest at "the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens"

    Focus Areas¶

    1. Priestly Qualifications — Levitical vs. Melchizedek: Tool discoveries show the PRIEST entry distinguishes pre-Mosaic priesthood (Melchizedek, GEN 14:18) from the Mosaic institution (EXO 28:1-4; NUM 3:10). The qualification requirements include Levite descent (NUM 3:5-10; 18:1-7), physical perfection (LEV 21:17-23), holiness (LEV 21:6,7; 22:9,16), and consecration (EXO 29:1-9,19-35; LEV 8:6-35). The research agent should retrieve all these passages with context and investigate how Christ fulfills these qualifications despite NOT being of Levite descent — Heb 7:13-14 explicitly acknowledges Christ is from Judah. The Melchizedek order (PSA 110:4; HEB 7:1-21) is the key to resolving this. Run cross-testament parallels on PSA 110:4.

    2. Consecration Ceremony — "Filling the Hands": The Strong's search revealed H4394 millu ("filling," i.e., consecration) as the technical term for priestly ordination. Exo 29:1-37 and Lev 8 describe the seven-day consecration ceremony. The research agent should retrieve EXO 29:1-37 and LEV 8 with full chapter context, parse the Hebrew of the key consecration verbs (qadash H6942, millu H4394), and trace how Christ's consecration differs (Heb 5:5-6; 7:28).
