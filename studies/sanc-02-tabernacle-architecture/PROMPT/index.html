    Key Findings from Related Studies:

    From sanc-01-why-a-sanctuary/CONCLUSION.md: - The sanctuary's stated purpose is divine dwelling: "that I may dwell among them" (EXO 25:8). The Hebrew verb shakan (H7931) denotes permanent, settled dwelling. - Dual naming: miqdash (H4720, from qadash "to be holy") names it by character; mishkan (H4908, from shakan "to dwell") names it by purpose. Together they pose the central question: how can a holy God dwell among unholy people? - Eden as proto-sanctuary is established through east-facing entrance (GEN 3:24 / EXO 27:13-14), cherubim guardians, precious materials, priestly vocabulary (le'avdah uleshomrah), and the tree of life. - The graduated holiness zones (courtyard -> holy place -> most holy place) control access to the increasingly concentrated divine presence. - Heavenly pattern: EXO 25:9,40; ACT 7:44; HEB 8:5; 9:23-24 -- Moses received a pattern (tabnith) from a heavenly original. - Glory-filling pattern ratifies each stage: EXO 40:34-35; 1KI 8:10-11; 2CH 7:1; REV 15:8. - The linguistic thread shakan -> mishkan -> Shekinah -> skenoo (G4637) bridges both testaments. - Open question identified: "How each specific element of the sanctuary furniture teaches the dwelling theology" -- directly addressed by this study.

    From revs-47-sanctuary-temple-typology/CONCLUSION.md: - Revelation uses exclusively naos (G3485, inner shrine, 16 occurrences) and never hieron (G2411, temple precincts). - Sanctuary compartmental distribution pattern (SP118): outer court elements (sacrifice altar) concentrate in seals, Holy Place elements (golden altar, incense, censer) concentrate in trumpets, Most Holy Place elements (ark, no-entry exclusion) appear at 7th trumpet and bowl prelude. - Day of Atonement progression (SP120): sacrifice (6:9) -> incense/censer (8:3-5) -> ark (11:19) -> no entry (15:8) -> linen vestments (15:6) -> wrath complete (16:17) -> scapegoat/abyss (20:1-3) -> no temple (21:22). - New Jerusalem as expanded Holy of Holies (cubic dimensions, REV 21:16 / 1KI 6:20). - Temple-to-Throne transformation: the mediating structure is transcended when God and Lamb ARE the naos (REV 21:22). - Light-word triad: lychnia (lampstand, REV 1-2), lampas (torch, REV 4:5), lychnos (lamp/light, REV 21:23; 22:5).

    5. The Three Compartments: Dimensions, Furniture, and Function - WHAT: Map the exact dimensions, furnishings, and function of each compartment: outer court (EXO 27:9-17; 38:9-16), Holy Place (EXO 26:31-37; 40:22-26; HEB 9:2), Most Holy Place (EXO 26:33-35; 40:20,21; HEB 9:3-5). The Nave's TABERNACLE entry organizes around three physical zones. Study 1 identified "graduated holiness zones" as a core architectural principle. - HOW: Retrieve EXO 25-27 (full chapters) for construction specifications, EXO 36-38 for construction execution, HEB 9:1-10 for NT interpretation.     6. The Bronze Altar and Laver: Outer Court Theology - WHAT: Investigate the bronze altar (EXO 27:1-8; 38:1-7) and laver (EXO 30:18-20; 40:30-32). Determine what the bronze material signifies, why these two articles are in the outer court, and what theological sequence they establish. - WHY: Nave's ALTAR entry shows the bronze altar was "a place of refuge" (EXO 21:14; 1KI 1:50). The LAVER entry reveals it was "made from the mirrors" of the women (EXO 38:8) and is figuratively linked to REV 4:6 and 15:2 (sea of glass). The WASHING entry links cleansing to regeneration (TIT 3:5; EPH 5:26). These outer court articles establish the first steps of approach: death (sacrifice) and cleansing. - HOW: Retrieve EXO 27:1-8 and EXO 30:18-20 with context. Look up H5178 (nechosheth, bronze) with search_strongs.py --verses to trace bronze usage. Run cross-testament parallels on EXO 30:18-20 (laver washing) to find NT cleansing parallels. Retrieve EXO 38:8 for the mirror detail.

    7. The Holy Place Furnishings: Lampstand, Showbread, Incense Altar - WHAT: Analyze the three furnishings of the Holy Place -- the golden lampstand (EXO 25:31-40; 37:17-24), the table of showbread (EXO 25:23-30; 40:22,23), and the altar of incense (EXO 30:1-10; 37:25-28). Determine what each represents and how they relate to ongoing relationship with God. - WHY: Nave's entries show: the lampstand was "called THE LAMP OF GOD" (1SA 3:3) and is symbolic in ZEC 4:2,11 and REV 1:12,13,20; the showbread was called "BREAD OF THE PRESENCE" (HEB 9:2) and was "before the Lord continually" (EXO 25:30); the incense altar's use includes intercession (EXO 30:7-10) and is seen in REV 8:3 as connected with "the prayers of all saints." The revs-47 study identified a light-word triad (lychnia -> lampas -> lychnos) tracing the lampstand's symbolism into Revelation. - HOW: Retrieve EXO 25:31-40 (lampstand), EXO 25:23-30 (showbread table), EXO 30:1-10 (incense altar) with full context. Run cross_testament_parallels on EXO 25:31 (lampstand -> ZEC 4, REV 1:12), EXO 25:30 (showbread -> JHN 6:35), and EXO 30:7-8 (incense -> REV 8:3-4). Look up the burning schedule: every night (EXO 27:20,21) and trimmed every morning (EXO 30:7).

    8. The Most Holy Place: Ark, Mercy Seat, Cherubim, and the Divine Presence - WHAT: Examine the Most Holy Place as the locus of God's presence: the ark of the covenant (EXO 25:10-22; 37:1-9), the mercy seat (EXO 25:17-22), the cherubim (EXO 25:18-20), and what was inside the ark (law, manna, Aaron's rod -- HEB 9:4). Determine what the arrangement teaches about God's character. - WHY: The Nave's MERCY-SEAT entry shows the shekinah dwelt upon it (EXO 25:22; LEV 16:2; NUM 7:89). The ARK entry lists contents: law (EXO 25:16,21; DEU 10:5), Aaron's rod (NUM 17:10; HEB 9:4), manna (EXO 16:33,34; HEB 9:4). Study 1 identified the mercy seat (kapporeth from kaphar, "to cover/atone") as the place where God meets His people -- the solution to the holiness-dwelling tension. - HOW: Retrieve EXO 25:10-22 (ark and mercy seat specifications), LEV 16:2,13-15 (Day of Atonement access), HEB 9:1-10 (NT commentary). Run hebrew_parser.py on EXO 25:22 ("I will commune with thee from above the mercy seat"). Run cross-testament parallels on EXO 25:22 and HEB 9:5.

    9. Material Progression: Bronze to Silver to Gold - WHAT: Trace the material gradient from outer court (bronze -- H5178 nechosheth) through the structural base (silver sockets -- H3701 keseph, EXO 26:19; 38:25-27) to the Holy Place and Most Holy Place (gold -- H2091 zahab). Determine whether this progression encodes theological meaning. - WHY: Strong's discoveries show H5178 (bronze) is the outer court material (altar, laver), H134 (eden, sockets) made of silver form the tabernacle's foundation (EXO 38:25-27 -- from redemption money), and H2091 (zahab, gold) covers everything in the Holy Place and Most Holy Place. The silver sockets are particularly significant because EXO 38:25-27 explicitly connects them to the atonement money of EXO 30:11-16 -- the redemption price. DAN 2:32-45 uses these same metals in a prophetic statue (gold head, silver chest, bronze thighs). Nave's GOLD entry shows figurative use (LAM 4:1; 1CO 3:12) and symbolical use (DAN 2:32-45; REV 21:18,21). - HOW: Retrieve EXO 38:24-31 (material accounting), EXO 30:11-16 (atonement money), EXO 26:19 and 36:24 (silver sockets). Look up H5178 (nechosheth), H3701 (keseph), H2091 (zahab) with search_strongs.py --lexicon for each. Run hebrew_parser.py on EXO 38:25-27 to examine the silver-redemption connection.

    10. Fabric Colors and Their Significance: Blue, Purple, Scarlet, White - WHAT: Analyze the four primary fabrics/colors used in the tabernacle: white linen (H948 buwts / H906 bad), blue (H8504 tekeleth), purple (H713 argaman), and scarlet (H8144 shaniy). Trace where each appears and what it represents. - WHY: Strong's searches identified all four color terms. Nave's LINEN entry links pure white linen to "righteousness" (REV 15:6; 19:8,14). EMBROIDERY entry shows blue, purple, and scarlet were intermingled on curtains (EXO 26:1,36; 27:16) and the high priest's garments (EXO 28:4,39). The outer court was surrounded by a white linen curtain (EXO 27:9), while the gate combined all four colors (EXO 27:16). The veil separating the Most Holy Place was made of blue, purple, scarlet, and fine twined linen with cherubim (EXO 26:31). - HOW: Retrieve EXO 26:1 (inner curtains), EXO 26:31 (veil), EXO 27:9-16 (courtyard curtains and gate), EXO 28:5-8 (priestly garments). Look up H8504 (tekeleth, blue), H713 (argaman, purple), H8144 (shaniy, scarlet), H948 (buwts, fine linen) with search_strongs.py --lexicon. Search for NT parallels to these colors.

    11. Acacia Wood Overlaid with Gold: The Incarnation Question - WHAT: Investigate the use of acacia (shittim) wood (H7848) as the structural material overlaid with gold (EXO 25:10-11,23-24; 26:15,29; 30:1-5). The acacia is a desert tree -- durable, resilient, thorny. The ark was acacia overlaid with gold (EXO 25:10-11), the table was acacia overlaid with gold (EXO 25:23-24), the altar of incense was acacia overlaid with gold (EXO 30:1-5), and the frame boards were acacia overlaid with gold (EXO 26:15,29). Study 1 established that "according to the pattern" means the earthly teaches heavenly realities (EXO 25:9,40; HEB 8:5). - HOW: Retrieve EXO 25:10-11 (ark), EXO 25:23-24 (table), EXO 30:1-5 (incense altar), EXO 26:15,29 (boards) for the wood+gold pattern. Run cross_testament_parallels on ISA 53:2 ("root out of dry ground" -- desert imagery?) and JHN 1:14 ("Word made flesh") to test the incarnation connection. Check whether any biblical text explicitly interprets the wood or gold symbolically.

    12. Directional Movement: East to West, Outer to Inner - WHAT: Map the directional theology of the tabernacle. The entrance faces east (EXO 27:13-14). Movement toward God's presence goes east to west, from the gate through the outer court, through the Holy Place, to the Most Holy Place. Determine what this movement teaches and whether Scripture interprets it. - WHY: Study 1 noted the east-facing entrance parallel with Eden (GEN 3:24 cherubim "at the east of the garden"). Nave's CHERUBIM entry confirms they were "eastward of the garden of Eden" (GEN 3:24) and embroidered on both curtains and veil (EXO 26:1,31). EZK 43:2,4 describes the glory returning from the east. The single entrance emphasizes one way of approach. HEB 9:1-10 describes the sanctuary as having a first compartment and then "after the second veil" the Most Holy -- a progressive movement inward. - HOW: Retrieve EXO 27:13-16 (east-facing gate), EXO 26:31-33 (veil between Holy Place and Most Holy Place), HEB 9:1-10 (NT commentary on the two compartments), HEB 10:19-22 (access through the veil as Christ's flesh). Run cross_testament_parallels on HEB 10:19-20. Also retrieve EZK 43:1-4 (glory entering from the east) and GEN 3:24 (cherubim at the east of Eden). Parse EXO 27:13 for directional terms.

    13. The Courtyard Fence: White Linen Enclosure and the Single Gate - WHAT: Examine the outer court enclosure -- white linen curtains on silver-capped bronze pillars (EXO 27:9-17; 38:9-16) and the single eastern gate (EXO 27:16; 38:18). Run parallels on JHN 10:9 and JHN 14:6 to test the "one entrance" theme.

Determine what the author of Hebrews says the tabernacle structure teaches. - WHY: HEB 9:1-5 provides the most detailed NT description of the sanctuary layout: first tabernacle with lampstand, table, showbread; then after the second veil, the golden censer, ark overlaid with gold, mercy seat, cherubim.
