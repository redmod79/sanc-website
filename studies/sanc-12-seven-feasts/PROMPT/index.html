    Bible Study: Seven Feasts as Prophetic Calendar¶

    Question¶

    What are the seven appointed feasts (mo'adim) of Leviticus 23, and do they form a unified prophetic calendar? What is the structure of the festival year and what does each feast typify?

    Key Findings from Related Studies:

    From sanc-11-two-goats-typology/CONCLUSION.md: - The two goats of Lev 16 constitute a single, indivisible sin offering (Lev 16:5) addressing propitiation through blood and elimination through removal - LORD's goat typifies Christ's sacrificial death and entrance into the heavenly sanctuary (Heb 9:12; Rom 3:25) - Scapegoat operates in two typological registers: Christ's sin-bearing (Isa 53:4,11; Heb 9:28) and Satan's millennial binding (Rev 20:1-3) - The hilasterion (G2435) lexical bridge: LXX kapporeth = hilasterion = Rom 3:25 = Heb 9:5 - Azazel is grammatically a proper noun (BHSA classification), parallel to YHWH in the lot-casting formula - Lev 16:20 vekhillah marker: blood-atonement MUST be complete before scapegoat phase begins - Sequence maintained: cross (propitiation) -> heavenly ministry -> second coming (Satan bound) -> final destruction - The two-bird ceremony (Lev 14:4-7) confirms the one-killed/one-released typological template

    From sanc-09-day-of-atonement-ritual/CONCLUSION.md: - kaphar (H3722) appears 16 times in Lev 16 -- more than any other chapter - Ceremony proceeds innermost to outermost: Most Holy Place -> Holy Place -> altar -> scapegoat -> outside camp - Two irreducible phases: blood-atonement (propitiation) and sin-removal (scapegoat) - Lev 16:17 total exclusion (kol-adam lo yihyeh) = chiastic center -- atonement is exclusively God's work through His mediator - White linen garments (bad, H906) = humility for atonement, with glory-humility-glory arc mapping to Phil 2:6-11 - Three categories of sin addressed: tum'ot (uncleanness), pesha'im (transgressions), chattotam (sins) - Primary object of atonement is the sanctuary itself, not the people directly (Lev 16:16) - Rev 15:5-8 extends the DOA "no man in the temple" pattern into eschatological judgment

    From law-24-weekly-sabbath-vs-ceremonial-sabbaths/CONCLUSION.md: - Lev 23:3 states weekly Sabbath BEFORE the annual feasts restart at v.4 -- structural separation - Lev 23:37-38 explicitly separates feasts from "the sabbaths of the LORD" using millibad ("beside/apart from") - Two distinct work prohibition levels: kol-melakhah (all work) for weekly Sabbath vs. melekhet abodah (servile work) for feast days - The weekly Sabbath is NEVER called a moed (appointed feast) or chag (pilgrimage feast) - Weekly Sabbath is "in all your dwellings" (bekhol moshboteikhem) -- NOT tied to central sanctuary - Col 2:16-17 "sabbath days" most naturally refers to ceremonial feast sabbaths (the triad feast/new moon/sabbath in 2Chr 31:3, Eze 45:17, Hos 2:11) - The weekly Sabbath continues into the new earth (Isa 66:23)

    From law-33-calendar-continuity/CONCLUSION.md: - The crucifixion-resurrection three-day sequence permanently anchors the Sabbath between Friday (paraskeue) and Sunday (first day of the week) - Fixed weekly day-names in Greek: paraskeue (G3904) = Friday/Preparation; prosabbaton (G4315) = day before Sabbath - This calendar framework is essential context for understanding festival timing relative to the weekly cycle - The festivals fell on specific calendar dates (Lev 23:5,24,27,34), not on specific days of the week -- contrasting with the weekly Sabbath

    Focus Areas¶

    1. Leviticus 23 as structural blueprint: Lev 23 is the definitive chapter listing all seven feasts in sequence. The research agent should retrieve LEV 23:1-44 with full text, noting the structural markers: the Sabbath frame (v.3), the introductory formula "feasts of the LORD" (v.2, v.4), and the closing summary (vv.37-44). Parse the Hebrew of key terms: mo'ed (H4150), miqra qodesh (H4744 + H6944), shabbathon, chag. WHY: Law-24 discovered that Lev 23 has a deliberate structure separating the weekly Sabbath from the feast cycle -- the research agent must map this structure precisely.

    2. Passover and Unleavened Bread (Nisan 14-21): The first feast cluster. Tool discoveries show 1CO 5:7 explicitly identifies Christ as "our passover." The research agent should retrieve LEV 23:4-8; EXO 12:3-28; NUM 28:16-25; 1CO 5:7-8; JHN 1:29; JHN 19:36 (none of his bones broken); MAT 26:17-28. Run hebrew_parser.py on LEV 23:5-8 for the ritual prescriptions. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py --hybrid-ot AND --hybrid-nt on 1CO 5:7 and JHN 1:29. WHY: Passover is the foundational feast, explicitly connected to Christ's death. The lamb-without-blemish typology needs verse-level documentation.

    3. Firstfruits (Nisan 16 -- "morrow after the sabbath"): The wave sheaf offering. Tool discoveries show LEV 23:10-14 prescribes waving the omer on the day after the Sabbath. 1CO 15:20,23 calls Christ "the firstfruits of them that slept." ROM 8:23 connects firstfruits to the Holy Spirit. The research agent should retrieve LEV 23:10-14; 1CO 15:20-23; ROM 8:23; JAS 1:18; MAT 27:52-53. Look up H1061 (bikkur, firstfruits) and H6016 (omer, sheaf) with search_strongs.py --lookup and --verses. Run hebrew_parser.py on LEV 23:10-11 for the wave-sheaf ritual. WHY: Christ's resurrection "on the morrow after the sabbath" (Sunday) directly fulfills the Firstfruits timing.

    4. Pentecost / Feast of Weeks (Sivan 6 -- 50 days after Firstfruits): The wheat harvest and two leavened loaves. Tool discoveries show ACT 2:1-11 records the outpouring of the Holy Spirit on Pentecost. LEV 23:15-21 prescribes counting 50 days. The two LEAVENED loaves (LEV 23:17) are unique -- all other grain offerings are unleavened. The research agent should retrieve LEV 23:15-21; NUM 28:26-31; DEU 16:9-12; ACT 2:1-21; ACT 2:41-47. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on ACT 2:1 and LEV 23:17. WHY: Pentecost is explicitly fulfilled in Acts 2. The leavened loaves possibly represent Jews and Gentiles brought together (both containing sin/leaven yet accepted). The 50-day count connects Firstfruits to Pentecost as Christ's resurrection to the Spirit's outpouring.

    5. The four-month gap between Pentecost and Trumpets: Between Sivan (month 3) and Tishri/Ethanim (month 7), there is a four-month interval with no feast. The research agent should note this structural gap. Retrieve JHN 4:35 ("four months, and then cometh harvest") and examine whether this corresponds to the church age between the spring feasts (first advent) and fall feasts (eschatological). Also retrieve ISA 18:4-5 (waiting before harvest). WHY: If the feasts form a prophetic calendar, this gap may correspond to the period between Christ's first and second advents.

    6. Feast of Trumpets (Tishri 1): The beginning of the fall feast cluster. Tool discoveries show LEV 23:24-25 prescribes a "memorial of blowing" (zikron teru'ah). The research agent should retrieve LEV 23:24-25; NUM 29:1-6; NEH 8:2,9-12; PSA 81:3-5; 1TH 4:16; 1CO 15:52; MAT 24:31; REV 8:2,6; REV 11:15. Look up H8643 (teru'ah) and H7782 (shophar) with search_strongs.py. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on LEV 23:24 and 1TH 4:16. WHY: Trumpets is the least explained feast in Leviticus -- it is given no stated reason, only "a memorial of blowing." The eschatological connections (trumpet of God, last trump, seven trumpets) need investigation.

    7. Day of Atonement (Tishri 10): Already studied in sanc-09 and sanc-11. The research agent should summarize established findings and focus on its POSITION in the feast calendar: 10 days after Trumpets, 5 days before Tabernacles. Retrieve LEV 23:26-32; LEV 16:29-34; HEB 9:7-12,24-28; HEB 10:1-4; DAN 8:14. Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on DAN 8:14 and HEB 9:24. WHY: The Day of Atonement is the only feast that requires affliction of soul (inuy nephesh) -- it is a solemn rather than joyful feast, representing judgment.

    8. Feast of Tabernacles / Ingathering (Tishri 15-22): The final feast, with the eighth-day solemn assembly. Tool discoveries show ZEC 14:16-19 prophesies nations keeping Tabernacles in the eschatological age. JHN 7:2,14,37-39 records Jesus at Tabernacles making the "rivers of living water" declaration. The research agent should retrieve LEV 23:33-44; NUM 29:12-38; DEU 16:13-15; ZEC 14:16-19; JHN 7:37-39; REV 7:9-17 (great multitude with palm branches); REV 21:3 ("the tabernacle of God is with men"). Run cross_testament_parallels_v2.py on ZEC 14:16 and REV 21:3. WHY: Tabernacles is the ONLY feast explicitly prophesied to continue into the eschatological age. Its connection to the harvest ingathering and dwelling in booths typifies the final ingathering of the redeemed.

    9. Colossians 2:16-17 -- "shadow of things to come": This verse explicitly calls the feasts (holiday, new moon, sabbath days) "a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ." The research agent should retrieve COL 2:16-17; HEB 8:5; HEB 10:1; ROM 5:14. Look up G4639 (skia, shadow) and G5179 (typos, type) with search_strongs.py --lexicon and --verses. Run greek_parser.py on COL 2:16-17 for precise grammatical analysis. WHY: This is the key NT passage that establishes the typological principle for the feasts -- they are shadows whose body/substance is Christ. Law-24 established that the "sabbath days" here refers to ceremonial feast sabbaths, not the weekly Sabbath.

    10. The agricultural calendar as prophetic framework: The feasts track the agricultural cycle: barley harvest (Passover/Firstfruits, spring), wheat harvest (Pentecost, early summer), grape/olive ingathering (Tabernacles, autumn). The research agent should retrieve JOL 2:23-24 (former and latter rain); HOS 6:3; JAS 5:7 (early and latter rain); ISA 9:3 (harvest joy); REV 14:14-20 (harvest and winepress). WHY: The agricultural cycle provides the natural framework for the prophetic calendar, with the harvest metaphors extending into eschatological judgment passages.
