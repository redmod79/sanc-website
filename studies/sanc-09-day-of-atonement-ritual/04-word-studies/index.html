    Word Studies¶

    kaphar (כָּפַר) — H3722¶

    Original: כָּפַר Transliteration: kâphar (kaw-far') Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 102

    Definition¶

    A primitive root; to cover (specifically with bitumen); figuratively, to expiate or condone, to placate or cancel: appease, make (an atonement, cleanse, disannul, forgive, be merciful, pacify, pardon, purge (away), put off, (make) reconcile(-liation).

    Translations (130 total occurrences in KJV)¶

    • "shall make an atonement" — 20x (15.4%)
    • "to make an atonement" — 18x (13.8%)
    • "and make an atonement" — 10x (7.7%)
    • "to make reconciliation" — 3x (2.3%)
    • "be purged" — 3x (2.3%)
    • Also translated: forgive, pacify, pardon, reconcile, cleanse, be merciful, disannul

    Key Verses — Leviticus 16 Occurrences¶

    H3722 appears approximately 16 times in Leviticus 16 (vv. 6, 10, 11, 16, 16, 17, 17, 18, 20, 24, 24, 27, 30, 30, 32, 33, 33, 34). The objects of atonement vary:

    Verse Object of Kaphar Translation
    16:6 for himself and his house "make an atonement"
    16:10 with/upon the live goat "to make an atonement with him"
    16:11 for himself and his house "make an atonement"
    16:16 for the holy place "make an atonement for the holy place"
    16:17 in the holy place / for himself, household, congregation "to make an atonement...have made an atonement"
    16:18 for the altar "make an atonement for it"
    16:20 made an end of reconciling the holy place, tabernacle, altar "reconciling"
    16:24 for himself and the people "make an atonement"
    16:27 in the holy place "to make atonement"
    16:30 for you, to cleanse you "make an atonement for you, to cleanse you"
    16:32 the atonement "shall make the atonement"
    16:33 holy sanctuary, tabernacle, altar, priests, people "make an atonement" (3x)
    16:34 for the children of Israel for all their sins "to make an atonement"

    LXX Connections¶

    Key Greek translations of H3722 in the LXX: - G2433 ἱλάσκομαι (hilaskomai) — "to conciliate, propitiate" (PMI: 6.47) — highest individual relevance - G2511 καθαρίζω (katharizo) — "to cleanse" (count: 14) - G863 ἀφίημι (aphiemi) — "to send away, forgive" (count: 14) - G129 αἷμα (haima) — "blood" (count: 12)

    Related Word Family¶

    • H3725 kippur (כִּפֻּר) — expiation/atonement (institution)
    • H3727 kapporeth (כַּפֹּרֶת) — mercy seat/covering (place)
    • H3724 kopher — ransom, covering price (cost)

    kippur (כִּפֻּר) — H3725¶

    Original: כִּפֻּר Transliteration: kippur (kip-poor') Part of Speech: Masculine plural noun BLB Count: 8

    Definition¶

    From H3722; expiation (only in plural): atonement. Gives the name "Yom Kippur" — Day of Atonements (always plural).

    All 8 Occurrences¶

    Reference Translation
    EXO 29:36 "of atonement"
    EXO 30:10 "of atonements" (sin offering of atonements)
    EXO 30:16 "the atonement"
    LEV 23:27 "of atonement" (day of atonement)
    LEV 23:28 "of atonement" (day of atonement)
    LEV 25:9 "of atonement" (day of atonement)
    NUM 5:8 "the" (the atonement)
    NUM 29:11 "of atonement" (sin offering of atonement)

    kapporeth (כַּפֹּרֶת) — H3727¶

    Original: כַּפֹּרֶת Transliteration: kappôreth (kap-po'-reth) Part of Speech: Feminine noun BLB Count: 27

    Definition¶

    From H3722; a lid (used only of the cover of the sacred Ark): mercy seat. Derived from kaphar — literally "the covering" or "the place of covering/atonement."

    Translations¶

    • "the mercy seat" — 22x (81.5%)
    • "of the mercy seat" — 3x (11.1%)
    • "a mercy seat" — 1x
    • "the mercy seatward" — 1x

    Leviticus 16 Occurrences¶

    • 16:2 — "before the mercy seat" / "upon the mercy seat" (2x)
    • 16:13 — "cover the mercy seat"
    • 16:14 — "upon the mercy seat eastward" / "before the mercy seat" (2x)
    • 16:15 — "upon the mercy seat, and before the mercy seat" (2x)

    LXX Connection¶

    H3727 kapporeth -> G2435 ἱλαστήριον (hilasterion) — "an expiatory (place/thing)" (PMI: 9.75, highest relevance) - This is the same word used in ROM 3:25 where Christ is called "hilasterion" — propitiation/mercy seat - The LXX bridge: kapporeth (OT mercy seat) = hilasterion (NT propitiation) = Christ (ROM 3:25) - Also: G2787 κιβωτός (kibotos) "ark" (PMI: 5.85), G2665 καταπέτασμα (katapetasma) "veil" (PMI: 7.44)

    dam (דָּם) — H1818¶

    Original: דָּם Transliteration: dâm (dawm) Part of Speech: Masculine noun BLB Count: 361

    Definition¶

    Blood (as that which when shed causes death) of man or an animal; by analogy, the juice of the grape; figuratively (especially in the plural) bloodshed: blood(-y, -guiltiness), (-thirsty), innocent.

    Translations¶

    • "the blood" — 79x (20.2%)
    • "blood" — 75x (19.2%)
    • Various prepositional forms (of/his/their/with blood) — remainder

    Leviticus 16 Occurrences¶

    • 16:14 — "the blood of the bullock" (2x) — sprinkled on/before mercy seat
    • 16:15 — "his blood" (goat's blood brought within the vail)
    • 16:18 — "the blood of the bullock, and of the blood of the goat" — on altar horns
    • 16:19 — "the blood" — sprinkled seven times on altar
    • 16:27 — "blood was brought in" — identifies which animals burned outside

    Key Theological Verse¶

    LEV 17:11 — "For the life of the flesh [is] in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it [is] the blood [that] maketh an atonement for the soul."

    nazah (נָזָה) — H5137¶

    Original: נָזָה Transliteration: nâzâh (naw-zaw') Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 24

    Definition¶

    A primitive root; to spirt, i.e. besprinkle (especially in expiation): sprinkle.

    Translations¶

    • "and sprinkle" — 8x (28.6%)
    • "And he shall sprinkle" — 3x
    • "shall sprinkle" / "shall he sprinkle" — 2x each
    • Various sprinkle forms — remainder

    Leviticus 16 Occurrences¶

    • 16:14 — הִזָּה (hizzah, Hiphil perf) — "and sprinkle" (on mercy seat eastward)
    • 16:14 — יַזֶּה (yazzeh, Hiphil impf) — "shall he sprinkle" (seven times before mercy seat)
    • 16:15 — הִזָּה (hizzah, Hiphil perf) — "and sprinkle" (goat blood on/before mercy seat)
    • 16:19 — הִזָּה (implied) — "shall sprinkle" (seven times on altar)

    Key Pattern¶

    The verb is always in the Hiphil (causative) stem in Lev 16, meaning "to cause to spatter/sprinkle." The sevenfold sprinkling (sheba' pe'amim) appears in v.14 and v.19, indicating completeness. This same verb appears in ISA 52:15 — "so shall he sprinkle many nations" — connecting the DOA sprinkling to the Suffering Servant.

    taher (טָהֵר) — H2891¶

    Original: טָהֵר Transliteration: ṭâhêr (taw-hare') Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 94

    Definition¶

    A primitive root; properly, to be bright; i.e. (by implication) to be pure (physically sound, clear, unadulterated; Levitically, uncontaminated; morally, innocent or holy): be (make, make self, pronounce) clean, cleanse (self), purge, purify(-ier, self).

    Leviticus 16 Occurrences¶

    • 16:19 — "and cleanse it" — the altar cleansed from uncleanness
    • 16:30 — "to cleanse you, [that] ye may be clean" (2x) — Piel infinitive construct + Qal imperfect — the PURPOSE of atonement: moral/ceremonial purification

    Key Verse: Lev 16:30¶

    Hebrew: לְטַהֵר אֶתְכֶם מִכֹּל חַטֹּאתֵיכֶם לִפְנֵי יְהוָה תִּטְהָרוּ "to cleanse (Piel inf. con.) you from all your sins; before YHWH you shall be clean (Qal impf.)" - The Piel (intensive) form shows God actively purifying - The Qal (simple) shows the resulting state: they SHALL BE clean

    qodesh (קֹדֶשׁ) — H6944¶

    Original: קֹדֶשׁ Transliteration: qôdesh (ko'-desh) Part of Speech: Masculine noun BLB Count: 468

    Definition¶

    From H6942; a sacred place or thing; rarely abstract, sanctity: consecrated (thing), dedicated (thing), hallowed (thing), holiness, (most) holy (day, portion, thing), saint, sanctuary.

    Translations¶

    • "holy" — 100x (22.1%)
    • "of the sanctuary" — 44x (9.7%)
    • "the holy" — 41x (9.1%)
    • "most holy" — 7x
    • "sanctuary" — 9x

    Leviticus 16 Occurrences¶

    • 16:2 — "the holy [place]" (ha-qodesh) — the Most Holy Place
    • 16:3 — "the holy [place]" — where Aaron enters
    • 16:4 — "holy linen coat" / "holy garments" (qodesh, 2x)
    • 16:16 — "the holy [place]" — object of atonement
    • 16:17 — "the holy [place]" — where atonement is made
    • 16:20 — "the holy [place]" — reconciling complete
    • 16:23 — "the holy [place]" — where garments changed
    • 16:24 — "the holy place" — where he washes
    • 16:27 — "the holy [place]" — where blood was brought
    • 16:32 — "the holy garments" (bigdei qodesh)
    • 16:33 — "the holy sanctuary" (miqdash ha-qodesh)

    anah (עָנָה) — H6031¶

    Original: עָנָה Transliteration: ʻânâh (aw-naw') Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 84

    Definition¶

    A primitive root; to depress literally or figuratively, transitive or intransitive: abase self, afflict(-ion, self), answer (by mistake), chasten self, deal hardly with, defile, exercise, force, gentleness, humble (self), hurt, ravish, sing (by mistake), submit self, weaken.

    Translations¶

    • "afflict" — 5x
    • "was afflicted" — 3x
    • "to afflict" — 2x
    • "to humble" — 1x
    • Also: force, humble, submit, chasten, weaken

    Day of Atonement Occurrences¶

    • LEV 16:29 — תְּעַנּוּ (Piel impf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"
    • LEV 16:31 — וְעִנִּיתֶם (Piel perf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"
    • LEV 23:27 — וְעִנִּיתֶם (Piel perf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"
    • LEV 23:29 — תְעֻנֶּה (Pual impf. 3fs) "shall not be afflicted" — PASSIVE: the soul that is not afflicted
    • LEV 23:32 — וְעִנִּיתֶם (Piel perf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"
    • NUM 29:7 — תְּעַנּוּ (Piel impf. 2mp) "ye shall afflict your souls"

    Key Notes¶

    The Piel stem (intensive active) is used for the command: actively, intentionally afflict. The Pual (intensive passive) in LEV 23:29 describes the person who fails to BE afflicted. The expression "afflict your souls" (te'annu et-nafshotekem) is unique to the Day of Atonement among all feast days. The word combines physical affliction (fasting) with spiritual humbling.

    karath (כָּרַת) — H3772¶

    Original: כָּרַת Transliteration: kârath (kaw-rath') Part of Speech: Verb BLB Count: 288

    Definition¶

    A primitive root; to cut (off, down or asunder); by implication, to destroy or consume; specifically, to covenant (i.e. make an alliance or bargain, originally by cutting flesh and passing between the pieces): be chewed, be con-(feder-)ate, covenant, cut (down, off), destroy, fail, feller, be freed, hew (down), make a league (covenant), lose, perish, utterly, want.

    Translations¶

    azazel (עֲזָאזֵל) — H5799¶

    Original: עֲזָאזֵל Transliteration: ʻăzâʼzêl (az-aw-zale') Part of Speech: Masculine noun (treated as proper noun in Hebrew parser) BLB Count: 4

    Definition¶

    From H5795 (ez, goat) and H235 (azal, to go away); goat of departure; the scapegoat: scapegoat.

    All Occurrences (4 total, all in Leviticus 16)¶

    • LEV 16:8 — "the other lot for the scapegoat" (la-azazel)
    • LEV 16:10 — "to be the scapegoat...for a scapegoat" (la-azazel, 2x)
    • LEV 16:26 — "the goat for the scapegoat" (la-azazel)

    Hebrew Parser Notes¶

    The Hebrew parser treats עֲזָאזֵל as a proper noun (PropN.ms.Abs), suggesting it may be a name rather than a description. Two main interpretations: 1. "Goat of departure" (ez + azal) — describing the goat's function 2. A proper name for a demonic entity sent to the wilderness — the goat is sent "to Azazel"

    In Lev 16:8, the parallel structure is significant: "one lot for YHWH (la-YHWH) and one lot for Azazel (la-azazel)" — the preposition la- ("to/for") is identical for both, suggesting Azazel is a personal entity (parallel to YHWH as a proper name), not merely a description of the goat's function.

REV 15:6 describes angels in "pure and white linen" — connecting to DOA white linen garments.

    qetoreth (קְטֹרֶת) — H7004¶

    Key Notes¶

    The incense (qetoreth) in the DOA is beaten fine (daqqah) and mixed with spices (sammim). The same word appears in Nadab and Abihu's unauthorized offering (LEV 10:1). The incense symbolizes prayer (PSA 141:2; REV 5:8; 8:3,4). The smoke filling the temple in REV 15:8 parallels the DOA incense cloud.
