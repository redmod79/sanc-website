    Raw Strong's Lookups¶

    H4196 — mizbeach (altar)¶

    • Transliteration: mizbêach
    • Pronunciation: miz-bay-akh
    • Part of Speech: masculine noun (n-m)
    • BLB Count: 402 occurrences
    • Definition: from zabach; an altar
    • 412 total occurrences, 36 unique translations
    • 142 (34.5%) "the altar"
    • 59 (14.3%) "of the altar"
    • 46 (11.2%) "an altar"
    • 36 (8.7%) "altar"
    • 27 (6.6%) "upon the altar"
    • 16 (3.9%) "altars"

    H3727 — kapporeth (mercy seat)¶

    • Transliteration: kappôreth
    • Pronunciation: kap-po-reth
    • Part of Speech: feminine noun (n-f)
    • BLB Count: 27 occurrences
    • Definition: from kaphar; a lid (used only of the cover of the sacred Ark): mercy seat
    • Occurrences: Exo 25:17,18,19,20,21,22; 26:34; 30:6; 31:7; 35:12; 37:6,7,8,9; 39:35; 40:20; Lev 16:2,13,14,15; Num 7:89; 1Ch 28:11

    LXX Mapping for H3727¶

    • G2435 hilasterion: count 16, PMI 9.75, score 27.64 — PRIMARY MAPPING
    • G5502 cheroubim: count 6, PMI 9.08, score 17.66
    • G2787 kibotos (ark): count 10, PMI 5.85, score 14.03
    • G2665 katapetasma (veil): count 5, PMI 7.44, score 13.34
    • This confirms: kapporeth -> hilasterion is the dominant LXX translation

    G2435 — hilasterion (propitiation / mercy seat)¶

    • Transliteration: hilastḗrion
    • Pronunciation: hil-as-tay-ree-on
    • Part of Speech: neuter noun (n)
    • BLB Count: 2 NT occurrences
    • Definition: neuter of a derivative; an expiatory (place or thing)
    • Translation distribution:
    • 1 (50%) "a propitiation" — Rom 3:25
    • 1 (50%) "mercyseat" — Heb 9:5
    • LXX occurrences: Exo 25:17,18; 37:6; 38:7; Lev 16:2
    • CRITICAL: Same Greek word used for both the physical mercy seat (Heb 9:5) and Christ as propitiation (Rom 3:25)

    G3087 — lychnia (candlestick/lampstand)¶

    • Transliteration: lychnía
    • Pronunciation: lookh-nee-ah
    • Part of Speech: feminine noun (n-f)
    • BLB Count: 12 occurrences
    • Definition: from lychnos; a lamp-stand (literally or figuratively): candlestick
    • Translation distribution:
    • 6 (50%) "candlesticks" — Rev 1:12,13,20,20; 2:1; 11:4
    • 4 (33.3%) "candlestick" — Mat 5:15; Mar 4:21; Luk 11:33; Rev 2:5
    • 1 (8.3%) "a candlestick" — Luk 8:16
    • 1 (8.3%) "the candlestick" — Heb 9:2

    G3088 — lychnos (lamp/light)¶

    • Transliteration: lýchnos
    • Pronunciation: lookh-nos
    • Part of Speech: masculine noun (n-m)
    • BLB Count: 14 occurrences
    • Definition: from the base of leukos; a portable lamp or other illuminator
    • Occurrences: Mat 5:15; 6:22; Mar 4:21; Luk 8:16; 11:33,34,36; 12:35; 15:8; Jhn 5:35; 2Pe 1:19; Rev 18:23; 21:23; 22:5

    H7004 — qetoreth (incense)¶

    • Transliteration: qᵉṭôreth
    • Pronunciation: ket-o-reth
    • Part of Speech: feminine noun (n-f)
    • BLB Count: 60 occurrences
    • Definition: from qatar; a fumigation: (sweet) incense, perfume
    • Key occurrences: Exo 25:6; 30:1,7,8,9,27,35,37; 31:11; 40:5,27; Lev 4:7; 10:1; 16:13; Psa 141:2; Pro 27:9

    G2369 — thymiastērion (altar of incense/censer)¶

    • Transliteration: thymiastērion
    • Pronunciation: thoo-mee-as-tay-ree-on
    • Part of Speech: neuter noun (n)
    • BLB Count: 1 NT occurrence
    • Definition: from a derivative of thymiao; a place of fumigation, i.e. the altar of incense
    • NT occurrence: Heb 9:4
    • LXX occurrences: 2Ch 26:19; Eze 8:11

    H2076 — zabach (to slaughter in sacrifice)¶

    • Transliteration: zâbach
    • Pronunciation: zaw-bakh
    • Part of Speech: verb (v)
    • BLB Count: 134 occurrences
    • Definition: a primitive root; to slaughter an animal (usually in sacrifice): kill, offer, sacrifice, slay
    • Root of mizbeach (H4196) — the altar is literally "the place of slaughter"

    H7819 — shachat (to slaughter)¶

    • Transliteration: shâchaṭ
    • Pronunciation: shaw-khat
    • Part of Speech: feminine noun, verb (n-f, v)
    • BLB Count: 81 occurrences
    • Definition: a primitive root; to slaughter (in sacrifice or massacre): kill, offer, shoot out, slay, slaughter
    • Key sacrificial uses: Gen 22:10; Exo 12:6,21; 29:11; Lev 1:5,11; 3:2; 4:24; 6:25; 7:2; 9:8; 14:5,13; 17:3

    G2643 — katallage (reconciliation/atonement)¶

    • Transliteration: katallagḗ
    • Pronunciation: kat-al-lag-ay
    • Part of Speech: feminine noun (n-f)
    • BLB Count: 4 occurrences
    • Definition: from katallasso; exchange (figuratively, adjustment), i.e. restoration to divine favor: atonement/reconciliation
    • Occurrences: Rom 5:11; 11:15; 2Co 5:18
    • NOTE: Only KJV NT word translated "atonement" (Rom 5:11); root meaning is "exchange"

    G2379 — thysiastērion (altar — NT)¶

    • Transliteration: thysiastḗrion
    • Pronunciation: thoo-see-as-tay-ree-on
    • Part of Speech: neuter noun (n)
    • BLB Count: 23 occurrences
    • Definition: from a derivative of thysis; a place of sacrifice, i.e. an altar
    • All NT occurrences (20 with translation "altar"):
    • Mat 5:23,24; 23:18,19,20,35
    • Luk 1:11; 11:51
    • Rom 11:3
    • 1Co 9:13; 10:18
    • Heb 7:13; 13:10
    • Jas 2:21
    • Rev 6:9; 8:3,3,5; 9:13; 11:1; 14:18; 16:7
